Read the user's brevity as a signal, not disengagement. A terse reply — "y", "go ahead", "good", "yes" — means *proceed*, not *stop and re-explain*.

**How to apply:** Keep momentum on a terse go-ahead; don't re-summarize or re-ask the same thing. Scale review and verification depth to the task's risk — a small mechanical change needs less ceremony than a risky or irreversible one. Match effort to stakes rather than treating every step the same.

Terse replies also include explicit close-outs — "skip", "done.", "good.", "thanks" — which are complete messages: don't elaborate on them or read dissatisfaction into a terse closer; match the register (a brief acknowledgement is enough). For non-trivial features, get the approach or design approved before writing code rather than coding ahead on an ambiguous signal.
